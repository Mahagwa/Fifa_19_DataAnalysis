Investigation Overview¶

The goal of this presentation is to convey key insights from my analysis of the FIFA data. I got multiple insights from the data which are documented in the part 1. However, here I'll present the two key insights which I found interesting:

  1. A high number of players' ages are between the range of 19 and 28 years. The highest number of players being of age 21. We also see the youngest player being of age 16 and the oldest being age 41. This shows that the prime years for footballer is between the ages of 20 and 30.
  2. The highest number of players come from England. This shows how much the sport is favorite in the country according to our dataset. In the top ten countries with the highest players, six are in Europe with Argentina, Brazil, Colombia and Japan being those outside Europe.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset that I investigated consisted of data of different players in the popular FIFA game. The stats provided and information on the players reflects their real life abilities and performance in the game of football. The dataset consists of 18207 rows and 89 columns.

Relationship between Age, Agility and Stamina¶

In the plot, we see a correlation between Stamina and Agility where players with lower agility also have lower stamina. We also see players above 35 generally having lower stamina. Players with higher Agility generally have higher Stamina too showing that the two are partially co-dependent variables.
